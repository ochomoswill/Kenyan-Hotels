Fairmont the Norfolk Hotel.

Fairmont The Norfolk continues to be Nairobi's finest and best-known luxury hotel. The hotel in Nairobi Kenya, is still the traditional starting point for safaris and the Lord Delamere Terrace is a popular meeting place, where drinks and light meals are served continuously from morning until midnight. A calm oasis in the heart of bustling Nairobi, the hotel features distinct restaurants and a bar, one to suit every mood and palate. Tatu, the award winning signature restaurant, Lord Delamere Terrace, the all day dining restaurant The Tea Room offers traditional English high-tea in a comfortable setting, whilst the elegant Cin Cin Wine Bar is the perfect spot to unwind, as you delight in the impressive wine selection. The Norfolk celebrated its centennial in 2004. The hotel in Kenya offers fine luxury accommodations in its 170 guest rooms and suites. The well appointed rooms and suites have been restored over the years to offer luxurious modern facilities, whilst maintaining their historic charm and unique personality.

Sarova Stanley Hotel.

Established in 1902, this luxurious hotel, host to Hemingway and Edward Prince of Wales, combines Victorian opulence, legendary service, and contemporary style with state-of-the-art facilities. The Sarova Stanley Hotel in Nairobi exudes colonial elegance. It houses the famous Thorn Tree restaurant, the Thai Chi restaurant with exquisite Thai food, and the Exchange bar, the site of the former stock exchange. The very large, beautifully furnished rooms have high-end bathrooms, scenic views, and satellite TV. Leisure options include a swimming pool, gym, and high speed internet access is available in the business center. Sarova Stanley Hotel is close to the lively restaurants and shops of Nairobi and only 10 miles from the airport.
